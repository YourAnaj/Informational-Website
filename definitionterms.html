Definition of Terms

This section explains key terms to ensure clarity and understanding throughout the document.

Angular Velocity (ω)

The rate at which an object (like a coil) rotates in a magnetic field. It is used to calculate the EMF in rotating coil systems.

Area (A)

The surface through which magnetic flux passes—usually the area of a loop or coil.

Coil Turns (N) / Number of Turns

The number of loops or turns in a wire coil. A higher number of turns increases the induced EMF.

Electromagnetic Induction

The process by which a changing magnetic field creates an electric current in a conductor. It is the principle behind generators, transformers, and other electromagnetic devices.

Electromotive Force (EMF)

The voltage generated by a change in magnetic flux in a coil. It is not a force, but a potential difference that can cause current to flow.

Faraday’s Law of Induction

A fundamental law stating that the induced EMF in a circuit is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux through the circuit.

Frequency (f)

The number of complete cycles or rotations per second, used to calculate angular velocity in rotating coils.

Induced Current

The electric current that flows in a circuit due to an induced EMF from a changing magnetic field.

Lenz’s Law

A law that states the direction of the induced EMF always opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it. This explains the negative sign in Faraday’s Law.

Magnetic Field (B)

A field produced by magnetic materials or moving electric charges. It represents the influence that produces magnetic force.

Magnetic Flux (Φ)

A measure of the total magnetic field passing through a given surface area.

Rotating Coil

A coil that spins in a magnetic field, which causes a changing magnetic flux and thus induces EMF according to Faraday’s Law.

Time Interval (∆t)

The duration over which the magnetic field changes, affecting the rate of change of magnetic flux and the resulting induced EMF.

Transformer

A device that uses electromagnetic induction to increase (step-up) or decrease (step-down) alternating current (AC) voltages efficiently.

Generator

A device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy using the principle of electromagnetic induction.
